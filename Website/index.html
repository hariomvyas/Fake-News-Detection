FAKE NEWS DETECTION

The public and academic communities have both expressed interest in the subject of fake news. Such false information has the potential to influence public opinion, giving malicious groups the chance to influence the results of public events like elections. Automatically identifying fake news is a crucial yet difficult topic that is still little understood since the consequences are so high. However, there are three features of false news that are universally acknowledged: the language of an article, the user responses it receives, and the source users endorsing it. The success and generality of the work that has already been done has been severely constrained by its heavy reliance on customizing solutions to a single criterion.

BACKGROUND / MOTIVATION

A very severe issue in our community is the exponential spread of fake news on social media. Typically, it is produced using the manipulation of photos, text, audio, and video. This suggests that a comprehensive approach is required for the detection of fake news. The use of social media for news consumption has pros and downsides. On the one hand, individuals seek out and consume news via social media due to its low cost, ease of access, and quick transmission of information. On the other side, it promotes the widespread dissemination of fake news, or low-quality news that contains deceptive information. The widespread dissemination of fake news has the potential to have very detrimental effects on people and society. As a result, the detection of fake news on social media has lately emerged as an area of research that is receiving a lot of interest. Social media brings unique characteristics and obstacles for fake news detection, rendering typical news media detection algorithms ineffective or irrelevant. Because identifying fake news on social media is a difficult and important topic, we did our project on Fake news detection. Additionally, we go over adjacent study fields, unresolved issues, and potential future research routes for fake news identification.

Model Development

Model development is an iterative process, in which many models are derived, tested and built upon until a model fitting the desired criteria is built. Subsequent modelling work may need to begin the search at the same place as the original model building began, rather than where it finished. Here we have developed multiple models. We have trained and tested them to find best among them.

We will approach this project utilizing the State of the Art techniques suggested by BERT and a unique encoder made available by Google called Universal Sentence Encoder. In addition, PyCaret, a classic Machine Learning tool, will be used. PyCaret is growing in popularity because to its user-friendliness and intriguing capabilities.

Future Work

The validity of information on the Internet has recently become one of the most important issue. By enabling users to freely exchange content, social networking sites have transformed the way that information is disseminated. It is nearly difficult to determine dependability in a timely manner due to the volume of information that is spread and the speed at which it does so, which emphasizes the necessity for automatic fake news detection systems. As discussed earlier, Fake news detection is such a sensitive problem which has a lot of consequences. So, it is very important to create a model which can help in detection of fake news. For the future work there are couple of things which can be done such as an application can be made in which with the help of API’s we can get the updates on the fake news which are determined by other software or applications that data can be retrieved and then used in our application. Also, in an application we can be a search bar in which certain values can be filled in different attributes such as date, Title, Subject, Place, and many more which can help to get the output of fake or True. Also, the information the application gets from other reliable sources can be ordered in a way where all the sources which are high rated and used majorly by the people can be considered legitimate which can ultimately help in news detection. With the help of NLP, we can create a model in which can predict the intention of the news that the intention is to spread something positive or negative. Also, on social media fake news is one of the biggest problems and to overcome that the application can be used through which the news which gets reported by a large number of people can be checked with the resources available and that can help to determine if the news is Fake or True and the news then can be deleted from social media.
